title: The game view
description: The game-state snapshot the engine sends in every StateUpdate.

import ProtocolType from "../../../components/ProtocolType.astro";

A [`StateUpdate`](/protocol/transport/) carries one field, `gameView` — a complete
`GameViewDto` snapshot of the game. It is the **only** carrier of authoritative
game state: the client throws away its previous view and re-renders the board
from each one.

:::note[Work in progress]
The `GameViewDto` is still a work in progress. We're working hard to get it right and lean.
:::

## GameViewDto

The whole-game snapshot: turn structure, both players, the battlefield, the stack,
and game-end state.

<ProtocolType type="GameViewDto" />

## PlayerDto

One player's public and private state — life, counters, and the cards in each of
their zones.

<ProtocolType type="PlayerDto" />

## CardDto

The engine's snapshot of a single card — every zone holds these. Only `id` and
`name` are needed to round-trip a choice; the rest drives richer rendering. The
prompt pages that show cards reference this same type.

<ProtocolType type="CardDto" />

## CombatAssignmentDto

A declared block: which blocker is assigned to which attacker.

<ProtocolType type="CombatAssignmentDto" />

## StackObjectDto

A spell or ability currently on the stack, with its targets.

<ProtocolType type="StackObjectDto" />

## StackTargetDto

One target chosen by a `StackObjectDto`, tagged with the [`TargetingIntent`](#targetingintent)
the engine inferred for it.

<ProtocolType type="StackTargetDto" />

## StackTargetKindDto

Which id space a [`StackTargetDto`](#stacktargetdto) points into — a card, a
player, or another object on the stack.

<ProtocolType type="StackTargetKindDto" />

## TargetingIntent

A semantic hint describing what an effect intends to do to its target — the
client uses it to pre-highlight sensible targets. Carried by [`StackTargetDto`](#stacktargetdto)
and by the targeting prompts.

<ProtocolType type="TargetingIntent" />
