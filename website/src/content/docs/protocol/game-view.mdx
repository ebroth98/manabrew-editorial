A [`StateUpdate`](/protocol/transport/) carries one field, `gameView` — a complete
`GameViewDto` snapshot of the game. It is the **only** carrier of authoritative
game state: the client throws away its previous view and re-renders the board
from each one.

:::note[Work in progress]
The `GameViewDto` is still a work in progress. We're working hard to get it right and lean.
:::

## GameViewDto

The whole-game snapshot: turn structure, both players, the battlefield, the stack,
and game-end state.

<ProtocolType type="GameViewDto" />

## PlayerDto

One player's public and private state — life, counters, and the cards in each of
their zones.

<ProtocolType type="PlayerDto" />

## CardDto

The engine's snapshot of a single card — every zone holds these. Only `id` and
the card's `identity` are needed to round-trip a choice; the rest drives richer
rendering. The prompt pages that show cards reference this same type.

<ProtocolType type="CardDto" />

## CardIdentity

Which card a `CardDto` or `StackObjectDto` is — name, printing
(`setCode`/`cardNumber`), and whether it's a token.

<ProtocolType type="CardIdentity" />

## CombatAssignmentDto

A declared block: which blocker is assigned to which attacker.

<ProtocolType type="CombatAssignmentDto" />

## StackObjectDto

A spell or ability currently on the stack. Its `targets` are
[`TargetRef`](/protocol/shared-types/#targetref)s, each tagged with the
[`TargetingIntent`](#targetingintent) the engine inferred for it.

<ProtocolType type="StackObjectDto" />

## TargetingIntent

A semantic hint describing what an effect intends to do to its target — the
client uses it to pre-highlight sensible targets. Carried by
[`TargetRef`](/protocol/shared-types/#targetref) and by the targeting prompts.

<ProtocolType type="TargetingIntent" />
